The Heart of Filipino Musical Tradition

Rondalla music represents the soul of Filipino cultural expression, weaving together Spanish colonial influences with indigenous musical traditions. Our ensemble from Victoria, Tarlac continues this legacy, preserving centuries-old melodies while introducing them to new generations.

Through our carefully curated performances and educational programs, we celebrate the intricate harmonies created by traditional string instruments, each contributing its unique voice to create the distinctive sound that defines Filipino Rondalla music.

Traditional Rondalla Instruments

Discover the unique characteristics and beautiful sounds of each instrument that creates the harmonious blend of Rondalla music

Bandurria

The soprano voice of the Rondalla, featuring 14 strings that produce bright, melodic tones. Essential for carrying the main melody in traditional Filipino folk songs.

Listen to Sample

Laud

The alto voice providing rich harmonic support with its 16 strings. Larger than the Bandurria, it adds depth and fullness to the ensemble's sound.

Listen to Sample

Octavina

The piccolo of the Rondalla, producing the highest pitched sounds. Its 14 strings create brilliant, sparkling tones that add brilliance to melodies.

Listen to Sample

Guitar

The rhythmic foundation providing chord progressions and bass lines. Essential for maintaining the harmonic structure of traditional Filipino folk music.

Listen to Sample

Ukulele

Adding a bright, cheerful tone to the ensemble. Its four strings contribute to the rhythmic accompaniment and melodic embellishments.

Listen to Sample

Double Bass

The foundation of the ensemble, providing deep bass tones that anchor the harmonic structure and give weight to the overall sound.

About Victoria Rondalla Ensemble

Founded in 1985 in Victoria, Tarlac, our ensemble has been dedicated to preserving and promoting Filipino musical heritage for nearly four decades.
